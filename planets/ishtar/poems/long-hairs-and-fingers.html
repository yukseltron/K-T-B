Long Hairs and Fingers

Long hairs and fingers like vines that crawl and descend. Punctured leaves and greenery with stripes flecked and stretched. Innocent of outdoor grit like those that grow on concrete. Cellular pipes and tubes for aquatic forms and nourishment from the chlorophyll. I see the green. I see it lurch to strangle and wrap. Victims beyond bugs like us and instead more meaty like we. Green stench and green mess. Dark and pulpy spinach trees, hairy and varied.

I find them boring. Left too long to sun bake they never wake again. Dry heat from the arid land and it dries up like paper. Uncaring and neglect an enemy to the thriving wash and banked light. We're going to sell them before they die off because the water is gone and the clouds are missing. Specimens taken to who knows where and why other than maybe to survive. Raw culprits for escaping the festering.

When I bought one, I thought she'd like it. It was ugly but cute too. Something gross but unique maybe like something I'd have seen on TV. Like in those cartoons where people bloat and wear no clothes. Not that I've seen them but I've seen people see them. Stuck in those lights and colours. Like the flowers on this thing. All weird and wrong. I really thought she'd like it. But you can't just make up for everything. You can't try so late.

Somehow, it ended up in the trash. I'm sure it was a mistake but it's still hard to not make me cry. I could see the flowers had curled. A mossy damp sweat of a scent. No lights or colours, just mundane and dying. I understood it. I felt the same. Curls uncurled and leaves furled. Sick green and yellow. Muck of a mess and mold. How did it ever survive out there before? How did I think it would be better with me? I got in there with it.

Cleaned it. Fixed it. Cut off those dead parts and baggage it couldn't carry anymore. It takes time but time comes regardless. Water showers and mist sprays to rinse the shame away. That cool stream is like a blanket on me. Holding me as I close my eyes and just sink. Wash that excess away, the dirt down the drain. Pool of cool in that shallow bath that hydrates and reanimates. I drink some as it slips past my lips.

Eventually, things get cold. It has passed me by but I've come along without realizing it. I see the thing now and it's not as ugly as I thought. That putrid and puke. That crawling. Maybe it was ok she didn't like it. Maybe I didn't have to get it. This place is no place for it. All too cold to grow wide and tall. Take me back to that jungle. Where the sun hides in trees and the air chokes you with phlegm. Take me back and leave me there again.
