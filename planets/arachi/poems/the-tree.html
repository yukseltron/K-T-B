The Tree

Sometimes, we feel compelled to follow an act to the finish. Maybe you don't even know what it is, just that the passion consumes you. It provides energy and you are its conduit to realization. That is how I felt when I saw this tree.

You would not believe me when I say it was made of gold. Not a fake, wooden gold. This was real, pure gold that came out as a sap. But I never knew this until after I felt a need to chop it down.

The sap hardened like ore, but the smell and taste remained that of gold. I was given a vision of what to make, and so I acted upon it. It will be this beautiful face, skin glistening with soft features. Its eyes would be most noble — yet inviting at the same time. This was the balance I strived. To that end, I was possessed.

When you saw it, you did not react as I wished. A revolting, disgusting, apathy. You spurned my work. Called it drôle and gauche. You refused to see it the way I did. In the end, the ridicule was too much. I abandoned my work. I left it to die.

Perhaps, I was wrong and you were right. Perhaps I wasn't seeing things your way either, and refused to see the problems. Now, it is clear as day. Each day, I see the same face over and over in my work. I see it staring back, no matter how hard I try to morph it. I've become imprisoned to my labour.

Go ahead, condemn me. It would serve either of us little purpose. The countless versions of my work are each lost to me. That is a regret we both now share. I can only hope those who come across it are given mercy.
