Cacophony

I hear the sharp swinging steel turbulence in my ears. It scrapes and stings and steals my shallow hearing. The decay will continue until deafness I fear. Then my spine. Tremor and vertigo with balance awry. Inescapable shakes infect all of my days. Cutless teeth and infirm bones rattle my head I cry. But would you ever. The sounds of existence traced by music and noise
