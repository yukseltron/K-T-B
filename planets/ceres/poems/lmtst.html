Last Middle Top Small Tall

It's called: The Last Middle Top Small Tall system. Now, if the name doesn't give it away, please know it's the best our team could come up. It's official name is lateral obfuscated obstaclizing peripheral artefact recovery ablution for bureaucratic legalese, what a mouthful!

To describe the system, Last Middle Top Small Tall (LMTST for short) is simply an intersection of LM and TS models for effective bureaucracy. Let me explain.

In the Last system, we simply go for the last file in the pile. This is also known as a "first-in, last-out", but we call it Last for short. In a Middle system, we grab the very middle file, rounding down if the total number of files is odd. A Top system then operates by grabbing the very top file. Not the first or last file, mind you, but whatever happens to be on top. This means we can randomly select a file, and place it on top. Is this cheating? No, because you don't know it happens. What about small and tall? I think those are self explanatory, small files first and then tall files first. Think of LMTST as BEDMAS. Last files first, then middle, then top, then small, then tall.

Why not a First Middle Bottom Large Short system (FMBLS)? Well, that'll have to be something you bring up in the next OKR.
