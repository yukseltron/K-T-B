RVNG pt.3

"— little sea born. Tried to take a biscuit. Couldn't one day..."

I barely pay attention. My mind's too occupied.

"...half of them don't even know. They'll never know..."

His chortled laugh unsettles me. I need time and peace to think about things. Five dead bodies found floating by. Executed.

"Still stuck! Been stuck for... I think maybe..."

I stand up.

"N-Now where ya going?"

I tell him I need to leave. Being here hasn't given me the clarity I'm looking for. Maybe nothing will, now that time's running out.

He shakes his head at me as I leave, breaking our ritual early.

"Is that it? There's only so much one can do."

Despite his old and frail self, his face is full of that familiar iron conviction. He can tell something's bothering me.

"Don't try anything or anyone you can't handle. I enjoy these talks. Even if it's mostly me."

I assure him things will be ok and leave. The sun glares down from the horizon as it sets in the distance. The air feels thick and the fields stay still.

For the first time, I feel afraid of the coming night. The hunt is different, and somehow I don't feel ready.
