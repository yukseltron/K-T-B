The Jailed Man

The jailed man finally came home. Or what's left of it. He did not get a welcome. Not that we didn't care, we were just caught by surprise. We had forgotten him and moved on. A criminal, a convicted murderer. What's there to welcome?

Kids still come to bother him though. An infamous killer of six teenagers. Cut them, gutted them, and strung em up high amongst the trees. Blood drenched the trees red. We had never seen such a thing happen here, in this small town. And now the jailed man has brought the curse back. Let out far too soon.

Luckily, curses pay well if you got the know-how. A troubled scene made for a popular tour. Maybe the bar is haunted. Maybe the kids still roam the woods nearby. Maybe he's still killing, during the cold low nights. The stories were for all the newfound tourists, but it wasn't long till us here got spooked by them too.

Not me though. I was one of those with the know-how. I set up some tours, I told them tales. I'd add to them too. What hurt could it do? Not much else interesting in this little town. But each passing year, some brash and ambitious visitor would arrive and demand more. More for their podcast, their videos, their channel, whatever. But one day, I met the most daring of them all.

"He's still around isn't he? Take me to him."

So I did, after a payment of course. I took her to the jailed man. His dilapidated home keeling over. I was shocked when he came out, and I became too afraid to move. The ghost stories had finally all hit me at once. But she ran to him, hugged him, and started to cry.

"I'm so sorry for what I've done."
