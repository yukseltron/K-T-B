"I'm looking at you, son. I'm looking past this —"

He points at his head.

"— and at this, this right here directly."

Emphatic pats on the chest.

"So please... just please do the same and be real with me on this. What happened on the night of August 3rd last week?"

The suspect just stares blankly before responding.

"Nothing, I don't know. I was with friends."

The detective recoils a bit at the flat answer.

"We've already asked those same friends who can't confirm anything. We know you're lying. But it's not too late. Just please, tell us the truth, son. I know it's in your heart to do so. I know that you didn't... uh, you don't — it's not your fault."

The suspect nods. However, the detective still needs a confession.

"But it seems you're hiding stuff from us. And I can reason. Trust me, I can be your friend. One you desperately need."

"I don't think so, I already told you. I have friends, I was hanging —"

The detective holds back his growing annoyance. But his patience is somehow a bottomless pit.

"Let's not bring up your friends, it's not helping your case. Let's instead try to be honest, yeah buddy? I see hope for you, and I just want you to take it. I am your guide, your guiding light. Please, just use me... and uh, get that salvation."

"No."

"Fine, go fuck yourself you miserable fuck face."

The detective leaves in a huff.
