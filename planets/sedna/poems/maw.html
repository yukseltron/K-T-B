Maw

They say you know a secret. One long hidden. A grand beast of revelations rests deep in a wakeless slumber. Punisher of the world. Could it be true? Old speaker, wise mystic, tell me your view.

He shakes, he spins. His eyes go backwards. A brisk smoke emerges from the embers and ashes. It envelopes us and transfigures him. I see but a visage of awful origin, engulfing me in its embalming presence.

I hear in my head its warning.

Little vermin, impish soul! You dare come here? Where I see, I control through whispers into your ear. Ten heads of your likes surrounding on spikes. Will you join them on your pike? Brash fool, tiny ant! Know your greed. I am power, raw and freed.
