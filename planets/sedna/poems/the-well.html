The Well

I hated my old home. For fifteen long years I had been kept there, in its aging wood and greying exterior. It was both a physical and mental prison, trapping me with my so called family. I would dream each night of a successful escape, but in reality the house was surrounded by an impenetrable forest, both opaque and leering. There was no way my child self would survive out there alone. The furthest I would go was a stone well perched on a hill right next to the tree-line. From there, I'd frighten myself by imagining shadowy figures catching me unaware. I never knew the real danger was hiding down in that deep dark well.

That well had been there for who knows how long. Its brackish and bitter water drunk by my whole family. It had a slimy flavour, a slightly mineral texture, and a metallic odour. Boiled and diluted, the water would remain unpleasant in appearance, but my father claims that's how it's been for generations. I suppose I am a failure for instead preferring to secretly collect rainwater for drinking. When they had found out, they hardly spared me their trouble.
