The Artefact

We're down past 4000 meters. This is the abyssal zone. Here, the depth crushes you in pitch black. The only companions you find here are forgotten creatures long untouched by sunlight.

The ground hits us. PSI stable. Our small beam of light is all we get. I catch from the corner of my eye, the flicking and flutter of goblinesque animals. They glow a soft blue as they whisk away. I'm not sure why, but I feel like they're guiding me.

I follow. I'm too curious. Eventually, I see a yellow flicker ahead. Approaching it, I recoil when I see a set of eyes staring sharply back at me. It looked alive for sure, but I soon realize it's just a statue in amazingly pristine condition. The retch of the salt water and kelp haven't clung to it at all. Immediately, I work to remove it. I have to study it.

Suddenly, water whirls around me with great force. I hear this decrepit laughter bleeding in my ears. I find myself sinking, unable to move. There's nothing I can do. There's no help around. I barely make a sound as I'm crushed into a sphere.

I awake. I'm back at my desk. The screen in front of me is my robot's camera, the artefact partly out of frame. Its ascent should only take a few more minutes. I calm myself — I'm safe, still here on the ship. Far from the deadly depths.

I step out onto the deck. I rub my eyes as I try to get that damn picture out of my head. The crane groans in rust and stops. It's arrived.
