My Brazen Friend

The refuse of society is where I belong. It's where I've always belonged. Cast away early in my life, I have always been garbage living in garbage. I see what you consider trash. My treasure.

I eat your food. Half moldy, half black from rot. I drink what little rain drips down here through the steep walls. I taste the lime, the salt, and the moss. I can't escape here. Nothing can or should.

Most of my day is spent digging. I dig where there's enough light shining through the gutter holes. Beacons guiding me to fresh new treasure. It's amazing what people throw away. How little they appreciate how much they truly have.

One day, I feel something different. It shines bright and is smooth yet strong. It feels the same as the coins I collect. But something felt different. This was much larger than any coin, it was almost as big as me. It even had an unlooking face.

I had never seen a face in so long. They were all lifeless before, yet this statue seemed alive if unmoving. I spoke to it. I gathered it food. I dragged it to my lair. It would be my best friend. My only friend down here.

It doesn't talk much, only the odd 'yes' or 'no'. It'll suffice. I ask it questions endlessly everyday — it seems to know the answer to almost everything. I never want to lose my friend.

"Are there people searching for you?"

"...Yes."

"Will they ever find us?"

"...No."

"You're my best friend. Am I yours?"

I see what almost looks like a smile on its face.

"...Yes."
