The Moon King

He saw right through me, eight palms each with nine eyes. Wings that fold endlessly upon themselves. Disappearing and reappearing anew. Scales of basilisks with a feathery underbelly. The maw gnaws its gaping jaws.

A wretchedness I feel scrambling around my neck. Silvery whispers of despair. The fading light corrupts itself to the blackening sky, grasping at me intently. Twin screeches chime together. Migraines rupture in my head. I stumble.

I hear the hollowed wail. A proclamation of its return. Long spent in deep slumber. It echoes and rushes up the steep stone temple steps in cataclysmic fashion. A low drum hums. A faint glow arises. I watch in fear. I watch in awe.
