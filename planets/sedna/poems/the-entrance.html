The Entrance

The desert is adorned with rocky blemishes, unnatural in their postures and positions. Their former features are carved away by erosions, scarred by the wind. Uncanny is how much they resemble statues balking at me. I can see the outlines of people in their last moments of animation. The blackness of the frozen rock capturing and corrupting their petrified moments of despair. All that is left is their bodies amalgamating back into stone. Their faces are wiped smooth and away. The desert sands slowly drown them.

The night sky showers me in darkness. I see but the faint glow of stars choking through the haze of storm clouds. Up ahead is a large pillar whose surface is covered with disorienting hieroglyphs. Each glyph seems to be an optical illusion of its own — woven tightly with fine lines and rings that seem to go on forever. My torch wanes as the cool desert air kicks up a hushing wind followed by a torrent of rain. Ahead of me appears an entrance of sorts. Rainwater pools in its grooves. Gateless and gaping, all it offers is a stairwell descending into a pitch black.

And without a choice, I enter below.
