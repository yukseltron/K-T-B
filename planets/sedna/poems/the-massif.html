The Massif

The pale horizon looms ahead. A bleached white landscape of cold ice and snow layered over ancient undisturbed rock. The mountains jut out and cut into the sky. Amongst them, the giant Gasherbrum lays down beautifully. The cold wind gives her shape as they dress her hills and brush her with powder. She whistles, and all of us can hear her waning song. The blizzard approaches.

He lies down in the battered tent. Its orange inner glow trapping what little warmth they have left. He's holding his hand and shaking violently as he tries to keep it in the nearly boiling pot of water. He can't feel anything except that throbbing numbness climbing up his arm and making him feel weak.

Looking in the pot, he can see a disgusting black charring around his fingers. His nails have a green hue and barely seem to be clinging on in their brittle state. The skin looks dark, twisted, and wrinkled into a burnt leather. Pus is forming at his knuckles where the gangrene stops. He'll lose those fingers, but he'll live.

"But she won't. Not out there for that much longer. You have to go back."

The storm doesn't relent in its fury. She barely managed to get him back here. It would be suicidal. No, the risk isn't worth it.

"She did it for you. She would do it again."

He can barely speak. But the words are true.

Against the howls and hail, against the bombards and blisters, she sets out into the storm once again.
