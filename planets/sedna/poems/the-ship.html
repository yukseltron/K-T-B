The Ship

It has been a week since we've last heard from the captain. He was a stern man, but my last memory of him was his muttering and pale look. It felt unusual then, but so was the discovery of the ship.

No one else seemed to recognize or react to the ship. It was another derelict, sails rotten and hull barnacled. Unspectacular if not ominous. Its name was gritted off, but it had a rather unique figurehead. It resembled a sort of serpent, and seemed to shine of gold. The lustre was enough to lure us in.

Despite the silence, boarding was smooth and uneventful. No crew, no ghosts, no log, no treasure. Some tried to salvage the grotesque figurehead, but they failed and we watched it fall into the ocean. I swear I saw it take one last breath before disappearing.

After that, we returned to our usual route. Vice captain's orders. He says the captain is terribly sick and in quarantine. Best not to bother him. But it's been days and no word.

In my nightmares, I keep seeing the same figurehead, its ugly serpentine face laughing at me as our stone ship sinks into its gaping mouth. Others have had the same dream and are convinced it has to be a curse from the derelict.

Yet, we are due for port at Messina tomorrow. The weather has been fair, and besides the captain everything seems fine. I believe us lucky. Perhaps it was better the figurehead wasn't in our hold.

— Signed, October 18th, 1347
